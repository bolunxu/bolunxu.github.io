Research

My research proposes algorithms and market designs towards an economic, reliable, and sustainable power system. I’m particularly interested in the following topics.

A Future with Energy Storage

Energy storage is the key to a low-carbon future for offsetting renewable fluctuations,
but managing these assets at the system level remains an open question. Dispatch decisions must be made in real-time to accommodate instantaneous imbalances, but also need to consider the value of stored energy in the future. Shaping future insights into a computation-viable form for real-time decision making is a challenge for many aspects in the power system, including automatic generation control and economic dispatch.

Individuals in the System

A power system is more than a physical infrastructure, it interconnects millions of individuals with different roles and objectives. A household with roof-top PV is both a consumer and a producer, but might not be an active participant in the system. Merchants like energy storage and financial entities are neither consumer nor producer, but they contribute flexibilities and liquidities that aid power system operations. Analyzing individual objectives and looping their behavior into the system design is of great importance.

Re-imaging Power System

With more inertia-free and distributed resources, the power system is transforming from a centralized sequential-stage scheme into a hierarchical structure with autonomous local agents and aggregations. This transformation poses a series of interesting research questions such as designing new market flexibility products and interfacing local aggregations with the wholesale market.
